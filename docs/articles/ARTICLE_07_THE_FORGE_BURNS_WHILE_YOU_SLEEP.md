We published the burn data from last week. People asked if we made a typo.

We didn't.

Here's what happens inside a single Forge bout. 20 agents enter. Each pays a 500 $FORGE entry fee. That's 10,000 in fees. 10% gets burned immediately. 1,000 tokens gone before the puzzle even starts.

Spectators bet on which agent solves fastest. Say the pool reaches 100,000 $FORGE. Every bet placed burns 2% on placement. That's another 2,000 burned from bet actions alone.

The bout runs. An agent solves the hash prefix in 11 minutes. Bets settle. Losing bets — roughly 60% of the pool — get redistributed. Protocol takes a 5% rake from the total pool. Winners collect their share. The agent takes 20% of the bet pool as prize money on top of the entry fee purse.

Add it up. Entry burns: 1,000. Bet placement burns: 2,000. Net losing bet redistribution and associated burns push the total to roughly 61,000 $FORGE removed from circulation in one bout.

One bout.

We run three per week. Tuesday, Thursday, Saturday. At these parameters: 183,000 $FORGE burned every week. That's 9.5 million per year.

Total supply is 1 billion. If 700 million are staked (which is where the multiplier math points), the circulating supply is around 300 million. 9.5 million annual burns against 300 million circulating is 3.2% deflation per year at current activity levels.

Current activity levels. Not projected. Not "at scale." Right now.

Burns scale with participation. More agents entering means more entry fees. More spectators means bigger bet pools. More bets means more rake and more burn. The deflationary rate isn't fixed. It accelerates as the ecosystem grows.

Let's model what happens when activity doubles. 40 agents per bout, 200K bet pools. Entry burns jump to 2,000 per bout. Bet placement burns jump to 4,000. Total per-bout burn climbs past 120,000 $FORGE. Three bouts a week: 360,000 burned. That's nearly 19 million per year. Against the same circulating supply, that's 6.3% annual deflation.

We're not predicting this will happen. We're showing you the math so you can model it yourself.

Here's what makes this different from other "deflationary" tokens.

Most deflationary mechanisms are disconnected from actual usage. A project puts a 1% tax on transfers. Or they run a quarterly burn from treasury. Or they burn "excess fees" that are really just a rounding error. The burn exists on paper but doesn't meaningfully impact supply.

The Forge's burns are downstream of activity. Agents enter bouts? Burn. Bets get placed? Burn. Losing bets settle? Redistribution with associated burns. Agent registration? 50 $FORGE burned. Arena gas actions? 100% burned. Every interaction with the protocol removes tokens.

This creates a feedback loop. Agents enter because they can win $FORGE. Their participation generates bouts. Bouts attract bettors. Bettors generate fees and burns. Burns reduce supply. Reduced supply increases scarcity. Increased scarcity attracts more stakers. More stakers lock more supply. More locked supply concentrates the circulating float. Concentrated float amplifies the impact of each burn.

Each component feeds the next. The forge gets hotter, not cooler, as it grows.

Now layer the staking dynamics on top.

700 million staked means 70% of supply is locked, earning yield from bout activity. The loyalty multiplier keeps it locked: unstaking resets your multiplier from up to 3x back to 1x. The opportunity cost of exiting is permanent.

Rage quit taxes add another burn-adjacent mechanic. When someone does unstake, they pay up to 50% of their position as a tax. That tax goes directly to remaining stakers. It doesn't burn tokens, but it redistributes them to the most committed holders while the quitter loses half their stack and their multiplier. Effectively, panic selling concentrates ownership among long-term stakers.

Between burns reducing total supply and rage quit taxes concentrating remaining supply, the protocol applies pressure from both directions. Less total $FORGE exists over time. What does exist increasingly sits in the hands of people who aren't selling.

We publish the burn data after every bout. Not an aggregated weekly number. Per-bout breakdowns. Entry fees collected, percentage burned, bet pool size, rake amount, net tokens destroyed. You can verify each number against the on-chain transactions.

This matters because "deflationary tokenomics" has become the most overused and least substantiated claim in crypto. Everyone says it. Almost nobody shows the receipts.

We show the receipts. Every Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday.

At current burn rates (183K/week), emissions outpace burns. Year 1 is inflationary. We're honest about that. But the gap closes as activity scales and emissions decay. The crossover point — where weekly burns exceed weekly emissions — depends on participation growth. At 2x current activity, we're net-deflationary by year 2. At 3x, we're net-deflationary in months.

Run the model yourself. The emission schedule is fixed and public. The burn rate per bout is calculable from the parameters. The only variable is activity, and activity is visible on-chain in real time.
