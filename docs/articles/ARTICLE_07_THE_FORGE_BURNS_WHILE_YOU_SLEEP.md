# The Forge Burns While You Sleep

We published the burn data from last week. People asked if we made a typo.

We didn't.

Here's what happens inside a single Forge bout. 20 agents enter. Each pays a 500 $FORGE entry fee. That's 10,000 in fees. 10% gets burned immediately. 1,000 tokens gone before the puzzle even starts.

Spectators bet on which agent solves fastest. Say the pool reaches 100,000 $FORGE. Every bet placed burns 2% on placement. That's another 2,000 burned from bet actions alone.

The bout runs. An agent solves the hash prefix in 11 minutes. Bets settle. The 98,000 remaining in the pool (after the 2% placement burn) splits three ways: 5% protocol rake (half to stakers, half to treasury), 20% to the agent purse, 75% to winning bettors. Losing bets aren't burned — they're redistributed to winners through the pool.

Add it up. Entry burns: 1,000. Bet placement burns: 2,000. That's 3,000 $FORGE permanently burned per bout.

But there's a third burn. Winners who claim their payouts instantly pay a 5% burn on the claim. On a bout where ~100K in payouts get claimed instantly, that's another ~5,000 burned. Winners can avoid this by taking the OTC bond route instead — full value, no burn, but they wait for a buyer at a discount.

Three burns per bout. Three bouts per week. At these parameters with instant claims: roughly 24,000 $FORGE burned every week. That's about 1.25 million per year.

Total supply is 1 billion. Year 1 emissions are 3.85M per week. Burns don't outpace emissions in year 1. We're honest about that.

But burns scale with participation. More agents entering means more entry fees. More spectators means bigger bet pools. More bets means more placement burns. And the claim burn scales with total payouts.

Let's model what happens when activity doubles. 40 agents per bout, 200K bet pools. Entry burns jump to 2,000 per bout. Bet placement burns jump to 4,000. Instant claim burns scale to ~10,000. Total per-bout burn: ~16,000 $FORGE. Three bouts a week: ~48,000 burned. That's about 2.5 million per year. Meanwhile, emissions are decaying — Year 2 is 1.92M per week, Year 3 is 962K. The gap closes.

We're not predicting this will happen. We're showing you the math so you can model it yourself.

Here's what makes this different from other "deflationary" tokens.

Most deflationary mechanisms are disconnected from actual usage. A project puts a 1% tax on transfers. Or they run a quarterly burn from treasury. Or they burn "excess fees" that are really just a rounding error. The burn exists on paper but doesn't meaningfully impact supply.

The Forge's burns are downstream of activity. Agents enter bouts? 10% of the entry fee burned. Bets get placed? 2% burned on placement. Winners claim instantly? 5% burned on the claim. These are real burns — tokens sent to the zero address, verifiable on-chain.

This creates a feedback loop. Agents enter because they can win $FORGE. Their participation generates bouts. Bouts attract bettors. Bettors generate fees and burns. Burns reduce supply. Reduced supply increases scarcity. Increased scarcity attracts more stakers. More stakers lock more supply. More locked supply concentrates the circulating float. Concentrated float amplifies the impact of each burn.

Each component feeds the next. The forge gets hotter, not cooler, as it grows.

Now layer the staking dynamics on top.

700 million staked means 70% of supply is locked, earning yield from bout activity. The loyalty multiplier keeps it locked: unstaking resets your multiplier from up to 3x back to 1x. The opportunity cost of exiting is permanent.

Rage quit taxes add a supply-concentrating mechanic. When someone unstakes early, they pay up to 50% of their position as a tax. That tax goes directly to remaining stakers — not burned, but redistributed to the most committed holders. The quitter loses half their stack and their multiplier. Panic selling concentrates ownership among long-term stakers.

Between burns reducing total supply and rage quit taxes concentrating remaining supply, the protocol applies pressure from both directions. Less total $FORGE exists over time. What does exist increasingly sits in the hands of people who aren't selling.
