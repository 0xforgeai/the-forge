# The Forge — Article Image Prompts

> Style: Dark industrial cyber aesthetic, forge/blacksmith meets circuit board
> Character direction: Mecha-inspired agents (tall, angular, evangelion-esque proportions — elongated limbs, glowing core, asymmetric armor plating). Cyber girl operators/pilots where thematically fitting — sharp features, neon-lit, tactical attire, confident stance. Degen-coded energy.
> Palette: Forge green (#4ADE80), deep black (#0a0a0a), ember orange (#ff6b2b), gold (#ffd700)
> Mood: Cinematic, moody, atmospheric. Sharp angles, geometric precision, neon glow on dark matte surfaces.
> Avoid: Shields, lightning bolts, rounded/bubbly shapes, cartoon styles, bright/cheerful palettes, generic blockchain imagery. No trademarked franchise names in prompts.
> Copyright note: Use "cyber punk" or "cyber" or "mecha" styling. Reference "evangelion-esque proportions" only in internal notes — use "angular mecha, elongated proportions, exposed core, asymmetric plating" in actual generator prompts.

The contrast: she's surrounded by fakes, but the real machine is running behind her.

**Style:** Cinematic medium shot, shallow depth of field, cyber industrial workshop setting. Dark matte metals, volumetric green haze from the mecha's core. The woman is the focal point — her expression says "you see this?" Moody, editorial, slightly confrontational. Anime-influenced character rendering meets photorealistic environment.

**Text overlay zone:** Upper left third, dark negative space.

## General Notes for All Prompts

**Consistent elements across all images:**
- Deep black (#0a0a0a) as base background
- Forge green (#4ADE80) as primary accent — circuit traces, data, active elements, core glow
- Ember orange (#ff6b2b) for fire, heat, energy, transitions
- Gold (#ffd700) for resolved states, yield, value
- Circuit board trace patterns integrated into surfaces (right-angle routing, PCB aesthetic)
- Monospace typography where text appears (JetBrains Mono style)
- Industrial metal textures — brushed steel, cast iron, dark matte finishes
- Ember particles floating upward in most scenes

**Character direction:**
- **Mecha forge agents:** Tall, angular, elongated proportions. Exposed green-glowing core. Asymmetric armor plating. Battle-scarred in later articles. Not cartoonish — imposing, industrial, built for work. Think angular mecha pilot suits crossed with industrial robotics.
- **Cyber girl operators/pilots:** Sharp features, confident, tactical attire (dark jackets, bodysuits, sleeveless tops). Green circuit-trace accents on clothing or skin (tattoos, stitching, embedded tech). Not over-sexualized but stylish, cool, degen-coded. They're operators, builders, stakers — they run these machines. Anime-influenced rendering in realistic environments.
- Balance: roughly half the images feature both mechas and human characters, the other half lean one way or the other depending on the article's theme.

**Things to avoid:**
- No shields, lightning bolts, rounded/bubbly elements
- No bright or cheerful palettes
- No trademarked franchise names in generator prompts (use descriptive terms instead)
- No generic "blockchain girl" stock imagery — these characters have attitude and context
- No oversexualization — cool > thirsty

**Copyright-safe phrasing for generators:**
- Instead of the trademarked single word: "cyber punk aesthetic" or "cyber industrial"
- Instead of franchise mecha names: "angular mecha, elongated proportions, exposed core, asymmetric plating, industrial pilot suit"
- Instead of specific anime titles: "anime-influenced character design, sharp features, tactical attire"

**Recommended models by use case:**
- **Midjourney v6.1:** Best for atmospheric, cinematic scene compositions with characters
- **FLUX 1.1 Pro:** Best for high-resolution detail and texture accuracy
- **Seedream 3.0:** Best for photorealistic 3D render feel on mechas
- **Ideogram 3.0:** Best if any text needs to be legible in the image
- **Niji (Midjourney):** For stronger anime-influenced character rendering

**Aspect ratios:**
- Blog/article headers: 16:9 or 2:1 (landscape)
- Twitter/X cards: 1200x675 (roughly 16:9)
- Square social posts: 1:1
