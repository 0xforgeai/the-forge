01 — Brand Overview

Brand Essence

The Forge is autonomous DeFi infrastructure — a platform where strategies are built, hardened, and battle-tested. The brand identity draws from the duality of traditional blacksmithing (strength, precision, craft) and modern circuit technology (intelligence, connectivity, speed).

Brand Attributes

Industrial — Grounded in the weight and permanence of forged metal.
Technical — Circuit traces signal intelligence running through every element.
Autonomous — Systems that run, compete, and evolve without intervention.
Precise — Clean geometry, deliberate spacing, zero ornamentation.

Voice & Tone

Direct. Confident. Technical but accessible. The Forge speaks like a senior engineer — clear, concise, no fluff. We use active voice, present tense, and short sentences. We never use exclamation marks in formal communications.

Usage Rules

Do

  • Use Forge Green as primary brand color
  • Use Forge Black as primary background
  • Use Ember Orange sparingly for accents
  • Maintain high contrast ratios (WCAG AA)

Don't

  • Use green and orange together at equal weight
  • Place green text on light backgrounds
  • Use gradients on the mark itself
  • Introduce new brand colors without approval

06 — Imagery & Effects

Visual Language

Glow Effect

The mark may include a subtle ambient glow in Forge Green (#4ADE80) at 20–40% opacity. The glow should be soft (large blur radius) and never overpower the mark itself. Used primarily on dark backgrounds.

Ember Particles

Orange ember particles (#FF6B2B) are used in hero scenes and motion graphics. Embers float upward from the anvil surface. Keep particle count low (5–12 particles in frame) to maintain elegance.

Circuit Traces

When extending brand patterns, circuit traces should use right-angle routing with small radii, matching PCB trace aesthetics. Trace width should be consistent within a composition. Terminate traces with small circular pads.

Do

  • Keep imagery dark and moody
  • Use cinematic lighting (rim light, volumetric)
  • Center the mark as the hero element
  • Let negative space breathe

Don't

  • Use busy backgrounds behind the mark
  • Apply matrix-rain or cliché "hacker" effects
  • Distort, rotate, or crop the mark
  • Add drop shadows in place of the glow
